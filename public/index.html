Hi, I'm Kyle!

Welcome to Kyle Compiles, a website I put together to introduce myself and make use of this catchy domain name that I've held dormant for far too long. I'm a software engineering leader with five years of experience in developing distributed web applications at scale and growing high-performing teams to do the same. My resume (available for download below) already has details about that, so I want to use this space to give a more personal, less keyword-oriented overview of who I am. A bit of background on me: I was born and raised in the Midwest. I always had an interest in technology which led me to study computer engineering and mathematics at the University of Iowa. After graduating in 2018, I moved out to Seattle with my partner and began working full time as a software engineer in the Last Mile logistics organization at Amazon. I spent the next few years developing some cool delivery planning systems before transitioning into management, where I built out a team of engineers to continue that work. After half a decade of figuring out better ways to get packages to your doorstep, I decided it was time for a change of pace, and I'm now on the search for my next adventure. This site is built using vanilla HTML/JavaScript and a little bit of Bootstrap CSS. It is hosted using Google Firebase and I manage the DNS through Google Domains. While I enjoyed making it, the skills I used are peripheral to my primary expertise, which is leading engineering teams in building scalable and extensible production-grade web applications (usually in Java or Python, usually on AWS). Most of the artifacts I've produced demonstrating this are unfortunately not mine to share, so please check my resume below for the highlights and contact me at anderslkyle@gmail.com to learn more!
